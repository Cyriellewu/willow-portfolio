About

I like finding the part of a problem that everyone has learned to ignore.

My work moves between research, systems, and product because the interesting questions tend to sit between them. A model can be accurate and still answer the wrong question. A product can be polished and still make an unearned claim. I am drawn to the work of making those gaps visible.

I am currently completing an MS in Computer Science at Columbia University on the Machine Learning track. Before that, I studied Data Science at Hong Kong Baptist University. I am looking for new-grad work where careful evaluation, system design, and product judgment all matter.

Research

I care about problem formulation, interpretable evaluation, and the difference between a strong metric and a useful result.

Engineering

I like explicit boundaries: what code guarantees, what models suggest, and how a system fails when the answer is uncertain.

Products

I make room for the parts of a human experience that are easy to discard: context, ambiguity, and the small evidence of a day.
