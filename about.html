About

I work on ML and product problems where evaluation, system boundaries, and user impact need to align.

My work moves between research, systems, and product. A model can be accurate and still answer the wrong question. A polished product still needs clear limits on what it can reliably claim. I build systems that make those assumptions and trade-offs inspectable.

I am completing an MS in Computer Science at Columbia University on the Machine Learning track. Before that, I studied Data Science at Hong Kong Baptist University. I move between ML research, full-stack product work, and the human decisions behind both.

Research

I care about problem formulation, interpretable evaluation, and the difference between a strong metric and a useful result.

Engineering

I like explicit boundaries: what code guarantees, what models suggest, and how a system fails when the answer is uncertain.

Products

I build products around context, ambiguity, and the information that conventional workflows often discard.

Side quests

Outside of coursework and research, I maintain AlphaWatch — a local-first investing companion combining market data, morning briefs, paper trading, and a quota-aware LLM router, designed to run at zero monthly cost. It is a Python/SQLite/Streamlit tool built for personal use, not a shipped product.
