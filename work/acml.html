Making predictive maintenance answer a decision, not a vague request.

An explainable dynamic graph-learning system for vehicle-chassis maintenance, designed around cost and diagnosis rather than a generic instruction to "predict failure."

Role
First author and lead researcher
Context
Published ACML research
Scale

Question Approach Evidence Limits

The question

What should a maintenance model optimize for?

"Predict failure" was too vague to be operational. I worked from noisy, scattered fault data toward tasks tied to the decisions people actually make: emergency-fee risk, cost tier, and subsystem diagnosis.

The approach

Use structure without losing the reason behind a prediction.

I developed NRP-GCN-RF: a GCN encoder paired with a Random Forest, over weighted fault graphs that combine temporal, spatial, and Apriori co-occurrence edges. Vehicle-wise chronological validation was used to prevent temporal and inter-vehicle leakage.

What changed

High-cost failure precision improved from 60% to 95%.

On the published task formulation, the system reached 98.9% fault-category accuracy, raised high-cost failure precision from 60% to 95%, and improved recall by 25%. Apriori rule mining made co-fault patterns inspectable rather than leaving the graph representation as a black box.

Publication
You Wu, Junwei Su, Sirui Zhang, Zhijian Li. "Explainable Dynamic Graph Neural Networks for Predictive Maintenance in Vehicle Chassis Systems." ACML 2025, Proceedings of Machine Learning Research, volume 304.
